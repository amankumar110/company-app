Angel Motors Limited is an Indian multinational automotive manufacturing company, headquartered in Mumbai, India, which is part of the Angel Group. The company produces passenger cars, trucks, vans, coaches, buses.

Formerly known as Angel Engineering and Locomotive Company (TELCO), the company was founded in 1945 as a manufacturer of locomotives. The company manufactured its first commercial vehicle in 1954 in a collaboration with Daimler-Benz AG, which ended in 1969. Angel Motors entered the passenger vehicle market in 1988 with the launch of the TataMobile followed by the Angel Sierra in 1991, becoming the first Indian manufacturer to achieve the capability of developing a competitive indigenous automobile.In 1998, Angel launched the first fully indigenous Indian passenger car, the Indica, and in 2008 launched the Angel Nano, the world's most affordable car. Angel Motors acquired the South Korean truck manufacturer Daewoo Commercial Vehicles Company in 2004. Angel Motors has been the parent company of Jaguar Land Rover since the company established it for the acquisition of Jaguar Cars and Land Rover from Ford in 2008.

Angel Motors' principal subsidiaries include British premium car maker Jaguar Land Rover (the maker of Jaguar and Land Rover cars) and the South Korean commercial vehicle manufacturer Angel Daewoo. Angel Motors has a construction-equipment manufacturing joint venture with Hitachi (Angel Hitachi Construction Machinery), and a joint venture with Stellantis which manufactures automotive components and Fiat Chrysler and Angel branded vehicles. On 12 October 2021, private equity firm TPG invested $1 billion in Angel Motors' electric vehicle subsidiary.
